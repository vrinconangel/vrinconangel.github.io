    "DevOps for the hybrid cloud"

    In this article IBM introduces us to the idea and implementation process of DevOps. the article begins by explaining the new era of development and oporations and just how essential it is for a company to integrate DevOps and cloud into their IT startegy. folowing this intoduction IBM share with us their point of view on DevOps. their first point; Adoption is expanding. into larger orginiations implamenting cloud. next, Hybrid is becoming the norm and this provides flexability in deployment. additionally, solutions vary by teams as different orginizations have different goal, orginization, cultures and tools. IBM also belives that app security is essential and should be integrated early and throughout the DevOps lifecycle. And finally continuos delivery is the desired outcome with the DevOps application.

    In the second part of this article IBM claims that the change in bussiness is driven by anylitics mobile and social technologies and is unpreccedented in speed and scope. the needs of customers constantly evolve. they give great examples of this including Uber, Spotify and other "born on the web" companys. IBM states that a company must innovate and adapt in order to keep up with all the consumers demands and they belive cloud is the best platform for software delivery. it allows for fater time to feedback from customers and the ability to monitor shifts in the market is key. all this change and all the frequent releases needs tighter collaberation betwrrn bussiness, development and IT opperations. and this is where DevOps come into play. DevOps has three main objectives. First, to speed continous innovation of ideas through collaberation development. second, to enable continous delivery of innovation by automating the software delivery process while eliminating waste. And third, to provide a feedback loop for continous learning from customers. all together the process of integrating DevOps follows these steps; Think, Code, Deliver, Run, Manage and learn.
